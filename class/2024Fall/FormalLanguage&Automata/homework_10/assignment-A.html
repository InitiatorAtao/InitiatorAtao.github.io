(Exercise 8.2.3) Design a Turing machine that takes as input a number \(N\) and adds \(1\) to it in binary. To be precise, the tape initially contains a $ followed by \(N\) in binary. The tape head is initially scanning the $ in state \(q_{0}\). Your TM should halt with \(N+1\), in binary, on its tape, scanning the leftmost symbol of \(N + 1\), in state \(q_{f}\). You may destroy the $ in creating \(N + 1\), if necessary. For instance, \(q_{0}\$10011 \vdash^{*} \$q_{f}10100\), and \(q_{0}\$11111 \vdash^{*} q_{f}100000\).

(Exercise 8.2.5 (b)) Consider the Turing machine \[M = \bigl( \{q_{0}, q_{1}, q_{f}\}, \{0,1\}, \{0,1,B\}, \delta, q_{0}, B, \{q_{f}\} \bigr).\] Informally but clearly describe the language \(L(M)\) if \(\delta\) consists of the following sets of rules: \[\begin{aligned} {2} & \delta(q_{0}, 0) && = (q_{0}, B, R),\\ & \delta(q_{0}, 1) && = (q_{1}, B, R),\\ & \delta(q_{1}, 1) && = (q_{1}, B, R),\\ & \delta(q_{1}, B) && = (q_{f}, B, R). \end{aligned}\]

\(L\left( M \right)=L\left( 0^{*}11^{*} \right)\) where the language on the right is expressed by regular expression.
