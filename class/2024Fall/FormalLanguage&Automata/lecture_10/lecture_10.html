Equalence of PDA and CFG

(Left sentential form) transition like \(P\rightarrow xA\alpha\), \(A\alpha\) called the tail.

From CFG \(G=(V,T,P,S)\) to PDA \(P=(\{q\},T,V\cup T,\delta,q,S)\). Where \(\delta\) is:

  1. For each variable \(A\), \(\delta(q,\epsilon,A)=\{(q,\beta)|A\rightarrow \beta \text{ is a rule of }\)G\(\}\).

  2. For each terminal \(a \in T\), \(\delta(q,a,a)=\{(q,\epsilon)\}\).

(CFG to PDA) CFG \(G=(V,T,P,S)\) and PDA \(P=(\{q\},T,V\cup T,\delta,q,S)\) above has \(N(P)=L(G)\).

Proof. (CFG to PDA) Suppose \(w\in L(G)\), \(w\) has a leftmost domination \(S\Rightarrow_{\mathrm{lm}} \gamma_1\Rightarrow_{\mathrm{lm}} \gamma_2\Rightarrow _{\mathrm{lm}}\cdots\Rightarrow _{\mathrm{lm}}\gamma_{n}=w\). We need \((q,w,s)\vdash ^{*}(q,y_1,\alpha_1)\vdash ^{*}(q,y_2,\alpha_2)\cdots\) where \(\alpha_i\) is the tail of \(\gamma_1\) (\(\gamma_1=x_1\alpha_1\)) and \(w=x_{i}y_{i}\). Then do induction on \(i\).

Suppose \(w\in N(P)\) or \((q,w,S)\vdash (q,\epsilon,\epsilon)\). To proof \(S\Rightarrow ^{*}w\), use induction on the number of moves on PDA to proof \((q,x,A)\vdash(q,\epsilon,\epsilon)\rightarrow (A\Rightarrow ^{*}x)\):

Basis: for 1 moves or \((q,a,a)\), \(a\Rightarrow a\) is trival.

Induction: Suppose \(A\rightarrow Y_1Y_2\ldots Y_{n}\), for each \((q,x_{i},Y_{i})\vdash(q,\epsilon,\epsilon)\) can lead to \(A\Rightarrow ^{*}w=x_1x_2\ldots x_{n}\). ◻

From PDA to CFG \(G\). Construct variable \([pXq]\) in \(G\) which refers to strings transfer PDA from state \(p\) to \(q\) and pop symbol \(X\in \Gamma\).

(PDA to CFG) For PDA \(P=(Q,\Sigma,\Gamma,\delta,q_0,Z_0)\), there is a CFG \(G\) s.t. \(N(P)=L(G)\) where:

Variables:

  1. S

  2. All symbols of form \([pXq],p,q\in Q,X\in \Gamma\).

Production:

  1. \(\forall p,S\rightarrow [q_0Z_0p]\)

  2. If \(\delta(q,a,X)\) contains \((r,Y_1Y_2\ldots Y_{n})\), then construct states \([qXr]\rightarrow a[rY_1r_1][r_1Y_2r_2]\ldots[r_{k-1}Y_{k}r_{k}]\).

Proof. (PDA to CFG) Induction to proof \((q,w,X)\vdash ^{*}(p,\epsilon,\epsilon)\Rightarrow ([qXp]\Rightarrow ^{*}w)\). and reverse. ◻

Deterministic PDA

(Deterministic PDA,DPDA) A PDA where:

  1. All \(\delta(q,a,x)\) is empty or singleton.

  2. If \(\delta(q,a,x)\) is not empty, \(\delta(q,\epsilon,x)\) is empty.

(Languages of DPDA) DPDAs accept languages which between RL and CFL.

Proof. (Languages of DPDA)

  1. For all RL, its DFA can be easily turned to DPDA by add default choices.

  2. Exist non-regular language like \(L=\{wcw^{R}|w\in \{a,b\}\}\) can be accepted by DPDA.

  3. \(L=\{ww^{R}|w\in \{a,b\}^{*}\}\) do not have a DPDA.

 ◻

DPDA with empty stack acceptance is weak, since any accepted string \(x\)’s prefix cannot be accepted because empty stack make no more moves. Languages like \(0^{*}\) cannot be accepted.

(DPDA to CFG) If \(L=N(P)\) for some DPDA \(P\), then \(L\) has an unambiguous grammar.

Proof. (DPDA to CFG) Juse use Theorem [theorem:PDA to CFG] (PDA to CFG) and deterministic feature. ◻
